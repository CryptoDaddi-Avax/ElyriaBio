These guidelines are part of the Elyria Bio Affiliate Agreement. They exist to protect you, your audience, and Elyria Bio. Because every product we sell is a research chemical for in-vitro laboratory use only, the way you describe and promote them is legally significant. Accepting these guidelines is a condition of participating in the program. Read them in full.

The one rule that can never be broken

Elyria Bio products are research-use-only (RUO) chemicals. You may never state, imply, or suggest that they are safe or intended for human or animal consumption, ingestion, injection, application, or any in-body use — and you may never provide dosing, administration, or protocol instructions.

Present these products as laboratory research materials only. A single non-compliant post can create regulatory exposure for you and for Elyria Bio, and is grounds for immediate termination and commission forfeiture.

Research-use-only framing

All promotion must position Elyria Bio products as reference materials for in-vitro laboratory research conducted by qualified professionals. Your content should make this context obvious, not bury it.

  • Describe products as "research chemicals," "reference materials," or "for laboratory / in-vitro research use only."
  • Include a visible research-use-only (RUO) statement wherever you promote, including video descriptions, captions, and pinned comments.
  • Focus on what is factually documented: HPLC purity, mass-spec identity, COA availability, lot traceability, cold-chain handling.

Prohibited claims

The following are never permitted in any affiliate content, in any format, including spoken video, captions, comments, DMs, and paid ads:

  • Human or animal use of any kind — consumption, ingestion, injection, dosing, "how to take it," cycles, stacks, or administration.
  • Medical or therapeutic claims — that a product treats, cures, prevents, diagnoses, or mitigates any disease, symptom, or condition.
  • Health / body outcomes — weight loss, muscle gain, anti-aging, healing, libido, energy, or other personal results.
  • Personal-use testimonials — "I tried it," before/after photos, or third-party human-experience stories.
  • Regulatory endorsement — implying FDA approval, clearance, or that the products are drugs, supplements, foods, or cosmetics.
  • Unverifiable quality claims — purity, potency, or safety figures beyond what the published certificate of analysis states.

Approved & prohibited language

Use these examples as a model. When in doubt, use the approved swipe copy provided in your portal — it is pre-cleared.

Say this

Approved"A research-use-only reference peptide. Every lot ships with a third-party COA reporting HPLC purity and mass-spec identity."
Approved"For in-vitro laboratory research by qualified professionals. Use code MARCUS60 to save."
Approved"I source my lab reference materials from Elyria Bio — documented, lot-traceable, cold-chain shipped."

Never this

Prohibited"Take 250mcg twice a week for best results."
Prohibited"This peptide helped me lose weight / heal my injury / sleep better."
Prohibited"Safe, FDA-grade, and ready to inject — the best GLP-1 for fat loss."

Required FTC disclosure

US law (FTC endorsement guides) requires you to clearly and conspicuously disclose that you earn a commission. The disclosure must be easy to notice — not hidden in a hashtag wall, not below the fold, and present in the same medium as the promotion.

Example: "Affiliate link — I earn a commission on qualifying orders at no extra cost to you. #ad"
  • Place the disclosure before the referral link or code, and say it aloud in video content.
  • Use plain terms ("ad," "affiliate," "I earn a commission"). Avoid vague tags like "#sp" or "#collab" alone.

Audience & targeting

  • Do not target minors. Your audience and platforms must be 18+ and appropriate for a research-materials context.
  • Do not market to patients, people seeking treatment, or audiences in a medical/health-outcome context.
  • Do not promote where prohibited by platform policy or by the laws of the audience's jurisdiction.

Honest marketing practices

  • No spam, unsolicited bulk email/DMs, or posting referral links in unrelated communities against their rules.
  • No cookie stuffing, forced clicks, fake or expired discount codes, or self-referral / fraudulent orders.
  • No bidding on Elyria Bio trademarks in paid search, or impersonating the brand on any channel.
  • State pricing and promotions accurately; do not invent claims about shipping, guarantees, or returns.

Brand & asset use

  • Use only the logos, banners, and copy provided in your portal's asset library, unaltered.
  • Get written approval before producing custom creative that names or depicts Elyria Bio.
  • Keep your content current — remove or update posts if a product, price, or COA changes.

Enforcement & consequences

We monitor affiliate content. Violations are handled based on severity. Any human-use, dosing, or medical claim is a severe violation and may skip directly to termination.

Step 1

Notice

Written warning and a required correction window for minor or first issues (e.g. a missing disclosure).

Step 2

Hold

Commission and payouts paused, non-compliant content must be removed before reinstatement.

Step 3

Termination

Account closed and unpaid commissions forfeited for severe or repeated violations.

Elyria Bio may, at its discretion, require removal of any content, withhold attribution on non-compliant orders, and report unlawful activity to the relevant platform or authority. You are responsible for your own content and for complying with all applicable laws in your audience's jurisdictions.

This document is an operational affiliate policy template, not legal advice. Before publishing, have it reviewed by qualified counsel against the regulations in your operating and target markets (e.g. FDA/FTC in the US and equivalent authorities elsewhere).
